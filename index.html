Our Story

The Story That Started This

We are Bill and Lili. He grew up in Illinois and Pennsylvania. She grew up in Kenya. We found each other, built a life together, and discovered something nobody warned us about. Even love has a default setting. And it is usually Western.

It started with game nights. Cards Against Humanity. Fibbage. Drawful. Someone would mention Dr. Who and the whole room would light up. Lili would smile and go quiet. Not because she didn't understand the words, but because she couldn't access the invisible layer underneath: the years of shared pop culture, the inside jokes of an entire civilization she hadn't grown up inside.

So she did what many people do when they don't want to look stupid in a room full of people who all seem to belong: she declined to play. Nobody called her rude. Nobody said anything unkind. But she felt it, that particular loneliness of sitting in a room full of laughter you cannot fully enter.

"I didn't grow up playing board games at all. In my family, time together looked different — cooking side by side, telling stories across generations, sitting with each other without needing a card to tell you what to say. Connection was the game. People were the point."

— Lili · Nairobi, Kenya

Bill kept suggesting more games, not realizing the problem wasn't the game he'd chosen. The problem was that no game had ever been built with her in mind. The realization was gradual. When games involved real conversation, Lili came alive. She leaned in. She had more to say than anyone in the room.

So Bill did something else instead. He learned her language. He studied Kenya's political history, the tribal relationships, the foods she grew up eating, the textures of her world. Not because he had to. Because he wanted to live inside her reality the way she had always been expected to live inside his. That is what brought them closer than any game ever had.

And it is exactly what we want to build, for every couple, every family, every person who has ever smiled and gone quiet in a room that wasn't quite built for them. We are not ready to tell you what we are making yet and that is intentional. We want to build it with your feedback, your stories, your experiences. We want your frustrations to become the mechanics and your traditions to become the cards. We are listening before we are building. That is a promise.

What We're Trying To Solve

The moments no one
has made a game for. Yet.

🎭

The "Subtitles" Moment

When your partner's family is all laughing and you're smiling along, understanding the words but not the joke. The cultural subtext that never got translated.

🪪

The "Which One Are You?" Moment

When your child, or you, gets asked to check one box, pick one side, claim one culture. As if identity worked like a multiple choice question.

🌍

The "Untranslatable" Moment

The word in your language that carries an entire philosophy. The concept your partner half-understands but has never fully felt. The thing you gave up trying to explain.

🕯️

The "Fading Tradition" Moment

The ritual your grandmother did, that your mother did less of, that you do almost never. The thing you're quietly afraid your children won't know at all.

⚖️

The "Whose Rules?" Moment

When a parenting disagreement isn't really about parenting. It's about two completely different inherited ideas of what "respect" or "family" actually means.

🏡

The "Where Is Home?" Moment

When someone asks and you pause — not because you don't know, but because the honest answer is too complicated for the small talk it was meant to be.

Before You Share

Our commitments to you.
